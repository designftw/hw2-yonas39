Graphic Design of MIT Sloan School Management Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion website

This section will evaluate and discuss the strength and weaknesses of the Graphic Design of MIT Sloan School Management Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion website
As per The Principle of Design there are multiple ways of analyzing a graphics design, i.e,.

  1. Alignment
  2. Repetition
  3. Proximity
  4. Contrast
  5. Simplicity

For the sake of this assignment, I will discuss about the alignment and repetition aspects of the MIT Management Sloan School Office of Diversity,Equity and Inclusion webpage.

Alignment

The MIT Sloan School Management Office of Diversity,Equity and Inclusion webpage did well in organization of the website. In its home page the name of the office was clearly written in a white color aligned to the center of the page with a beautiful colorfull background image. Right under the page title there is a small box that contain 4 directory elements that also is aligned to the center. Following the directory elements box there are texts, images and list of people. All texts are aligned to the center except the one's that needs some emphasis. As of Feb 20, 2023, the website gives an emphasis to a certain texts breaking the center alignment. Although this breaks the alignment rule it is a great way to catch users attendtion. Similarly, they used the same technique when they introduce team member or a certain important info i.e, image.

The texts are center aligned, at times they break the alignement rule to add emphasis on some texts

Repetition

The other aspect that worth discussing about the web page is its consistency and repetition. I manage to go through the website and and tried to observe their design concept. It was impresive how they are consistent and repetitive through out the website. if you click on each of the selection directory from homepage you will see all of them followed the center alignment and the intensional misaligned pieces. In adition, they used all the same method when they introduce an image to the website. either they put the image on the left and write discription to the right of an image or they put the image and description followed under the image. it make it easy to follow along for the users and give a good structure for the website.
I also noticed a good thing that was was very consistent and repetitive throughout in the webpages found in our team section. In this section they chose to put the headshot images of the staff members and put it in a circle and then a small Discription about the person to the right. Name set to be Bold and larger font size. If you become curious and want to learn more about the person and click on learn more option that is found under the discription then you will be redirected to a new page that has the MIT dom as a background image and the circle head shot image of the person you want to learn more about. this page also followed the center alignment and display the name of the person bold and larger font, contact info and their title, and about (simple bio). This is consistent for all the individuals I looked up. I would recommend if they keep the proximity for the learn text a bit closer so users can find it easily.
